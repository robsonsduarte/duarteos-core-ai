## CONTEXT

The social media app Telegram has over 900 million users around the world. Its founder Pavel Durov sat down with Tucker Carlson at his offices in Dubai for his first on-camera interview in almost a decade. The interview aired on April 16, 2024, as Episode 94 of The Tucker Carlson Show. Duration: approximately 1 hour.

---

## INTERVIEW CONTENT

### PART 1: Early Life and Background

[SUMMARY — from Novinite, Roya News]

Durov discussed his upbringing between Leningrad (now Saint Petersburg), Italy, and Russia. Born in 1984 in the Soviet Union, Durov moved to Italy at the age of four with his family (his father was an academic). He later returned to Russia.

---

### PART 2: Creating VKontakte (VK)

[SUMMARY — from The Moscow Times, Babel.ua]

Durov co-founded VKontakte (VK) in 2007, which became the largest social network in Russia, often called "Russia's Facebook."

---

### PART 3: Russian Government Pressure — Protests of 2011-2012

[SUMMARY — from The Moscow Times]

During the 2011-12 mass protests against rigging in Russia's parliamentary elections, Durov refused state orders to take down opposition communities on VKontakte.

---

### PART 4: Euromaidan and Leaving Russia (2013-2014)

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — The Moscow Times]
"Wait a minute, this is a different country. We won't betray our Ukrainian users because you asked us to do that."

[CONTEXT: In 2013, during Ukraine's Euromaidan protests, the Russian government requested that Durov share Ukrainian pro-democracy protesters' personal data from VKontakte.]

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — The Moscow Times]
"We decided to refuse and that didn't go [over] too well with the Russian government as well."

[SUMMARY — The Moscow Times]
Durov said a similar Russian request during Ukraine's Euromaidan protests left him with "two sub-optimal options": complying or resigning as CEO of VKontakte and fleeing Russia. He chose the latter, leaving Russia in 2014.

---

### PART 5: The Idea for Telegram

[PARAPHRASE — from web search excerpt of HappyScribe transcript]

Durov explained that the idea for Telegram came when they were still based in Russia. At some point they had a very stressful situation where armed policemen would come to his house, trying to break in, because he refused to take down opposition groups. He realized there was no secure means of communication. He wanted to tell his brother what was going on to coordinate, and every tool available was not secure or encrypted.

His brother Nikolai, being a genius programmer, was able to create the encryption standard they are still using to this day, with minor changes.

---

### PART 6: World Tour — Searching for Headquarters

[SUMMARY — from SF Standard, Babel.ua]

After leaving Russia, Durov went on a world tour with his team to find the right headquarters for Telegram. His stops included Berlin, London, Singapore, and San Francisco.

#### On Berlin/Germany:

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"The bureaucratic hurdles were just too difficult to overcome."

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"I was bringing the best-in-class programmers in the world to these places, and I was trying to hire them from a local company and the responses I got in places like Germany for example is that you can't hire people from outside the European Union."

#### On San Francisco:

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"San Francisco we really thought it would be the place for us to be in because all the tech companies are there or around."

[CONTEXT: Durov described visiting San Francisco and meeting with Twitter co-founder Jack Dorsey in 2014.]

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"[They] tried to grab my phone from my hands as I was tweeting that I had just met the founder of Twitter. They probably didn't expect resistance, so I snatched my phone back. There was a short fight with the guys and a little bit of blood involved, but I managed to run away."

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"It was a shock to me because I've traveled a lot and that was the first place I got attacked."

[CONTEXT: After leaving a meeting with Jack Dorsey, Durov was attacked by three men who tried to steal his phone on the street in San Francisco.]

---

### PART 7: Choosing Dubai

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"It's a neutral country. It's a small country that wants to be friends with everybody and is not aligned geopolitically with any of the big superpowers. It's the best place for a neutral platform like ours to be in if we want to make sure we can defend our users' privacy and freedom of speech."

[SUMMARY — The Moscow Times]
He and Telegram relocated to Dubai in 2017 to avoid being "geopolitically aligned" or "select winners in any political fight."

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — The Moscow Times]
"I would rather be free than to take orders from anyone."

---

### PART 8: Telegram's Company Structure and Management

[SUMMARY — from Babel.ua]

Durov described Telegram's unusually lean company structure:
- Durov is the sole owner and project manager
- Approximately 30 engineers on staff
- No HR department
- No plans for a public offering (IPO), as shareholders would slow development

---

### PART 9: Telegram's Growth

[SUMMARY — from Babel.ua]

Telegram registers 2.5 million new daily users organically with zero marketing spend, while competitors invest billions in advertising. At the time of the interview, Telegram had 900 million monthly active users. Durov said he expected Telegram to reach 1 billion users within one year.

---

### PART 10: Apple and Google — Platform Control

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — Babel.ua]
"When it comes to free speech, basically these two companies can censor everything you can read or access on your phone."

[SUMMARY — Babel.ua]
Durov expressed frustration with tech giants' control over mobile platforms, noting that Apple and Google can remove apps arbitrarily and interpret their own rules favorably to themselves.

---

### PART 11: FBI Recruitment Attempt

**Pavel Durov:** [Direct quote — SF Standard]
"They were trying to persuade him to use certain open-source tools that he would then integrate into Telegram's code that in my understanding would serve as backdoors."

[SUMMARY — Babel.ua, The Moscow Times]
Durov claimed that U.S. agencies (FBI) attempted to recruit a Telegram engineer during a visit to the United States, seeking information about the app's internal workings and trying to convince the engineer to include surveillance tools in Telegram's code.

---

### PART 12: Political Pressure from Both Sides in the U.S.

[SUMMARY — from Babel.ua]

Durov described receiving contradictory demands from U.S. politicians — a Democratic congressman requested Capitol riot participant data, while a Republican congressman warned against releasing it. This illustrated how Telegram faces pressure from opposing political sides simultaneously.

---

### PART 13: Denying State Control of Telegram

[SUMMARY — from The Moscow Times]

Durov denied that Telegram was Russian state-controlled, suggesting that Western competitors had spread this rumor to hurt Telegram's reputation. He emphasized Telegram's independence from any government.

---

### PART 14: Lifestyle, Wealth, and Freedom

[PARAPHRASE — from Novinite, web search summaries]

Durov revealed a deliberate aversion to ostentatious displays of affluence. He prefers a lifestyle free from the trappings of luxury, eschewing real estate, vehicles, and other status symbols in favor of a nomadic existence that aligns with his vision of personal freedom and independence.

Durov stated that for him, it was never about money. He noted he has a few hundred million dollars in his bank account or in Bitcoin since about 10 years ago and doesn't do anything with it — he doesn't own any real estate, jets, or yachts, and doesn't think that lifestyle is for him.

When asked directly about owning assets, Durov clarified he owns no land or real estate, explaining that his number one priority in life is freedom, and that once you start buying things, it ties you down to a physical location.

Durov holds multiple passports and residency permits, living a borderless, nomadic lifestyle for over a decade.

---

### PART 15: Elon Musk and X (Twitter)

[SUMMARY — from Babel.ua]

Durov expressed admiration for Elon Musk's purchase of Twitter/X and supported his innovative approach to platform development.

---

### PART 16: Telegram's Future and Vision

[SUMMARY — from multiple sources]

Durov expressed his vision for Telegram as a platform that transcends political divides and fosters open dialogue, while maintaining its commitment to user privacy and freedom of speech. He emphasized Telegram's neutrality in geopolitical affairs.
