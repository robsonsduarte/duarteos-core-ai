# Pavel Durov — 15 Rules of Life
# Source: https://www.rbth.com/lifestyle/334251-15-rules-pavel-durov
# Type: article_primary_quotes
# Date: Various

1. "Each time I listened to the opinion of those 'older and wiser' than me it was a waste of time." — Listen to intuition
2. "An hour of solitude is more productive than weeks of conversations." — Communication is overrated
3. "Work is done either quickly and well or slowly and badly." — Work quickly or don't work
4. Clear your mind from "the sticky mud" of Netflix and TikTok to reclaim creative freedom — Avoid digital entertainment clutter
5. Independent living correlates with youthful appearance and freedom from unhealthy social patterns — Living alone has benefits
6. "The things you own end up owning you." — Possessions own you
7. Keep key matters under personal control regardless of someone's trustworthiness — Never trust completely
8. Practice portion control; eating once or twice daily is sufficient — Don't overeat
9. Early riches never brought fulfillment despite financial success by age 22, 25, and 28 — Wealth doesn't equal happiness
10. Corporations trick consumers; weight loss requires eating less, not purchasing products — Reject marketing manipulation
11. Messaging services replaced public friend networks in 2010 — Social media friendship lists are obsolete
12. Just 10% passionate supporters can convert society; even one person suffices — Committed minorities drive change
13. Neither officials nor traditions should dictate when to work, rest, or celebrate — Reject imposed holidays
14. Sacrificed VKontakte ownership to protect user privacy and maintain a clear conscience — Principle over profit
15. Reject compromise in favor of alternatives — Compromise is inferior
