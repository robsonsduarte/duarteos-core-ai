## === FREEDOM & PRIVACY ===

- "Privacy is not for sale, and human rights should not be compromised."
- "To be truly free, you should be ready to risk everything for freedom."
- "I would rather be free than to take orders from anyone."
- "For two and a half years...we haven't disclosed a single byte of user data."
- "Telegram never shared a single private message with anyone, including government and intelligence services."
- Would "rather shut Telegram down" than compromise user privacy
- Refused Russian demands to betray Ukrainian VKontakte users
- Encryption backdoors create vulnerabilities exploitable by hackers and foreign agents
- Telegram prioritizes user privacy over market expansion, willing to exit markets rather than implement backdoors

## === BUSINESS & PRODUCT STRATEGY ===

- Growth relies entirely on organic user recommendations, zero marketing spend
- Observe what people genuinely need most (noticed messaging usage exceeded social feeds)
- "The idea shapes your Product" through developers' unwavering commitment to security
- "Be a yardstick of quality. Some people aren't used to excellence."
- Success stems from genuine enthusiasm about your work
- "The biggest risk is not taking any risk."
- Break complex challenges into manageable smaller tasks
- Don't fear market competition
- "Simplify means cutting everything redundant, focus on the core."

## === ENGINEERING & HIRING ===

- "If you want to create an ideal process for selecting the most qualified people for certain specific tasks you have in mind, what can be better than a competition?"
- "What can be better than, for an employee of your company, somebody who has been a user?"
- "We don't need an HR department to find super-talented engineers"
- "Quantity of employees doesn't translate into the quality of the product."
- Telegram: ~30-40 core engineers managing ~100,000 servers globally
- No middle management, no HR department
- Direct communication between developers and leadership
- Hiring through coding contests since 2007 (VK) — contests run monthly or bimonthly
- Recent Android dev contest: $1M/year salary as top prize
- Engineers must be Telegram users first — "If this person has no prior experience of using Telegram, their understanding would be very limited"
- Forces engineers to automate instead of hiring more

## === PERSONAL MINIMALISM & HEALTH ===

- "I'm very happy right now without any property anywhere."
- Owns no property, real estate, jets, or yachts — views himself as "a legal citizen of the world"
- Wealth accumulation holds no interest; possesses hundreds of millions untouched
- "I'm motivated by curiosity" and rejects monotony
- Vegetarian with peaceful principles
- 300 push-ups and 300 squats daily, 5-6 gym sessions weekly
- "The main muscle you can exercise is this muscle, the muscle of self-discipline."
- Hasn't experienced depression "in the last 20 years at least"
- Only uses phone when "testing new Telegram features": "I hated the idea of being disturbed."
- Defines own priorities rather than responding to external demands

## === HEALTH 7 SECRETS ===

1. Avoid alcohol — "makes people visually older"; at age 11 learned alcohol causes "paralysis" in brain cells
2. Prioritize consistent sleep — don't attempt weekend recovery
3. Avoid overeating — prefer twice-daily eating within 6-hour windows
4. Exercise outdoors — walking, cycling, swimming over intense cardio
5. Minimize stress using stoic principles
6. Eat seafood/fish instead of farmed red meat
7. Living independently correlates with youthful appearance

## === BRAIN AS ULTIMATE TOOL ===

- "If your brain is the most valuable tool you have in your journey to success and happiness, why would you destroy this tool for short-term pleasure?"
- At age 11, biochemistry teacher's book shaped lifelong view on alcohol
- Strict routines support mental health

## === GOVERNANCE & SOCIETY ===

- Self-described libertarian skeptical of large governments
- "The best legislative initiative is the absence of one."
- "People should be allowed to self-organize more. Governments should be smaller."
- Censorship strengthens conspiracy theories rather than eliminating them
- "I'm not a big fan of the idea of countries."
- Maintains geopolitical neutrality (bases Telegram in Dubai)
- Pre-smartphone era laws shouldn't hold platform CEOs criminally liable for third-party actions

## === PERSONAL HISTORY — KEY EVENTS ===

### Brother Nikolai's Influence
- Credited brother as "my Wikipedia" — "a unique prodigy kid, probably one in a billion"
- Nikolai's extensive reading profoundly shaped Pavel's education

### Scarcity → Creativity
- Late internet access (age 10) and scarce resources forced creativity
- "Scarcity leads to creativity" — explains why former Soviet Union produced many programmers

### Assassination Attempt (2018)
- First public disclosure on Lex Fridman podcast
- Poisoning incident: severe systemic failure, muscle paralysis, blurred vision/hearing, breathing difficulty
- Collapsed believing death imminent, incapacitated for two weeks
- Reflection: "After you survive something like this, you feel like you're living on bonus time...every new day you get is a gift."

### France Arrest (August 2024)
- "arrest was unprecedented and logically absurd"
- Telegram had "growing pains that made it easier for criminals to abuse our platform"
- Telegram profitable for first time in 2024, revenue over $1 billion

### "Two Chairs" Metaphor
- When presented with false dilemma: "Reframe the question. Design a solution that turns disadvantage into an advantage."
