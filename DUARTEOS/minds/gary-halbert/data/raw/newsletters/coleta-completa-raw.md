Tuesday, 5:41 p.m. June 12, 1984

Dear Bond,

This letter is going to be the first in a long series of letters in which I will attempt to communicate to you a lot of the important things I have learned in the last 46 years.

I am going to try to teach you what I have learned about selling by mail, getting and staying healthy, how to get along with people, and, in general, how to have a good life without getting yourself all screwed up.

There will also be stuff about sex, drugs and rock and roll!

I'm going to try to write to you every day of the week (except Sunday) and spend about one hour on each letter. That way, by the time I get out of here we should have covered a lot of ground.

Dear Bond,

No messing around. I'm going to dive right back into the subject of becoming a "student of markets".

"If you and I both owned a hamburger stand and we were in a contest to see who would sell the most hamburgers, what advantages would you most like to have on your side?"

The answers vary. Some people say they would like to have the advantage of having superior meat. Others say sesame seed buns. Others mention location. Someone usually wants the lowest prices.

"O.K., I'll give you every single advantage you asked for. I, myself, only want one advantage and, if you will give it to me, I will whip the pants off of all of you when it comes to selling burgers!"

"What advantage do you want?" they ask.

### The 10 Possibilities (List Hierarchy from worst to best):

**Possibility #1** - Names from a telephone book. TERRIBLE. Too much waste circulation. "This is like shooting with a shotgun instead of a rifle."

**Possibility #2** - Phone book names from high income areas. "A little better, but not nearly good enough."

**Possibility #3** - Known high-income professionals (doctors, lawyers, architects). "Not bad. We are now getting into areas where we at least have a chance."

**Possibility #4** - Upper income proven mail order buyers. "Now we are getting down to business."

**Possibility #5** - Wealthy people who ordered a similar investment book by mail. "Bingo! Now we're cooking."

**Possibility #6** - Same as #5 but REPEAT buyers. "Yeah! Now we're cooking!"

**Possibility #7** - Same as #6 but who PAID BIG MONEY. "Goody."

**Possibility #8** - Same as #7 but who bought VERY RECENTLY. "This is almost as good a list as we can get."

**Possibility #9** - Same as #8 AND the list broker says it's "WORKING LIKE CRAZY" for similar offers.

**Possibility #10** - YOUR OWN CUSTOMER LIST! "All other things being equal, your own customers should respond far better than any other list you can get. Of course there is one caveat. THEY MUST BE SATISFIED CUSTOMERS!"

"When you get stuck or emotionally jammed up one of the ways to get yourself unclogged and flowing again is just to keep moving. Run. Walk. Jog. Write. Do the dishes. Or whatever. But don't sit around waiting for a flash from Heaven. The key is movement!"

**Step 3** - Get as many DM pieces and space ads on the subject as possible.
**Step 4** - Review your notes, read headlines again.
**Step 5** - Create your product: extract the best ideas, arrange logically, start writing.

"Now, I don't know what that idea will be but let's say you have figured out how to personalize and customize..."

### Chapter 11 - The A-Pile / B-Pile Concept:

"It is my contention that everybody divides their mail every day into two piles. An 'A-Pile' and a 'B-Pile'. The 'A' pile contains letters that appear to be personal. Like letters from friends, relatives, business associates."

"The 'B' pile contains those envelopes that obviously contain a commercial message."

"Everybody always opens all of their 'A' pile mail."

"Our first objective is to get our envelope into the 'A' pile. And, it's so easy to do! All we have to do is make the envelope look personal."

"Let's get a little plastic baggie and put some dirt in it and then attach it to the top of our letter."

### Chapter 12 - Intimacy in Letters:

"When you tell the day of the week plus the exact time you are writing the letter, it makes it seem a more important communication. Sort of like a telegram. A time-dated communication carries considerable more weight than one which is not."

"Another additional way to achieve this bond of intimacy and immediacy in your letters is to describe where you are and what you are doing as you are writing the letter."

"'Dear Bond' gets a certain quality of focused attention that is not enjoyed by 'Dear Sir' or 'Dear Occupant' or 'Dear Reader'."

"Taking the reader by the hand and leading him exactly where I want him to go."

Saturday, 2:30 P.M. September 1, 1986

### The Genesis Story:

"I first got bitten by the mail order bug in the mid-1960's. What really got me fired up was reading a little set of booklets called The Direct Mail Guides written by a man named Thomas Hall."

"I read everything I could get my hands on about advertising, direct mail and mail order. I mean everything. If my local library or bookstore didn't have a marketing book I wanted, I would drive hundreds of miles to get it."

"I was driven. I read it all. I was voracious. I went on a reading frenzy with the same passion a Great White goes on a feeding frenzy."

### The "Life or Death" Thought Experiment:

"Gary, what would you do if you had to make your next mailing work? What if you could only mail one letter and, if you didn't get a response, you would quite literally, be beheaded?"

This reasoning led to ALL the A-Pile innovations:
- First class stamps (not bulk)
- No teaser copy
- Handwritten/typed address (no labels)
- Plain white envelope
- No brochure inside - just a LETTER

### The Coat of Arms Story:

"That letter generated 7,300,000 cash-with-order customers and was the wellspring that built a mail order company (Halbert's) that eventually employed more than 700 people, 40 of whom were needed just to make our bank deposits which often consisted of some 20,000 checks per day."

### The Fake Computer Letter Innovation:

"I found myself reading a newspaper story that changed my entire life. It was about a little old lady who had found a neat way to supplement her social security income" - she made coat-of-arms drawings.

Gary took this idea, extracted surnames from phone books, typed one letter per surname ("Dear Mr. Anderson"), had them offset printed as FAKE computer letters, and:

"We took in MILLIONS And MILLIONS And MILLIONS And MILLIONS And MILLIONS Of Dollars!"

### A-Pile / B-Pile Lecture (Full Version):

"Everybody in the world divides his mail into two piles which I call the A-Pile and the B-Pile."

"The most important thing you can ever do when creating a direct mail promotion is to make sure your letter gets in the A-Pile!"

"The first thing a person will do with all this stuff is sort through it to see if he has received any A-Pile mail. After that, then he throws it away!"

### The Million Dollar Example:

"I was asked to go to Las Vegas to help a guy who was in trouble. The guy was a nephew of Colonel Sanders."

"I hardly rewrote the promotion at all; I just cleaned it up a little so it flowed a little more smoothly. But, of course, the main thing I did was reformat his promotion."

Result: "That promotion is pulling in approximately $96,000.00 more every month. That's over one million extra dollars per year in return for 45 minutes' work."

### Seduction Analogy:

"It's a matter of timing. Selling is like seduction. If you ask a woman to go to bed with you, whether or not she says 'yes' is largely a matter of when you ask her. If you ask her immediately, as soon as you've been introduced, most likely she will think you are a creep. However, if you wait a while, wait until she gets to know you a little, wait until you've wined and dined her a few times... then your chances of getting a 'yes' for an answer are much, much better."

### The "Do It By Numbers" Method:

"If you have a good product or service, you probably already know exactly how to sell it when you are talking to someone during a 'one-on-one' situation."

"Most people who are very good in a personal selling situation 'freeze up' when it comes to transferring their sales pitch to the printed word."

### The Process:
1. Record all your phone sales conversations
2. Transcribe the best one
3. Number each paragraph
4. Rate each paragraph 1-10 for persuasive power
5. Cut them out, divide into "benefits" and "interesting facts"
6. Throw away anything rated 5 or below
7. Rewrite (but don't change much - just clean up)
8. Write it as a MEMO to a specific person you can visualize

## FRAMEWORKS E METODOS IDENTIFICADOS

### 1. A-Pile / B-Pile System
- Dividir o correio em pessoal (A) e comercial (B)
- Objetivo: fazer sua carta entrar no A-Pile
- Elementos: envelope simples, selo real, endereco escrito a mao, sem teaser copy

### 2. Starving Crowd Principle
- Encontrar mercados famintos ANTES de criar produtos
- Hierarquia de 10 niveis de qualidade de lista
- Recency > Frequency > Unit of Sale

### 3. The 7-Step Copy Sequence
1. Atencao
2. Interesse (expandir CSI)
3. Credibilidade
4. Prova
5. Beneficios
6. Como pedir
7. Pedir AGORA

### 4. Grabbers System
- Anexar objetos fisicos ao topo da carta
- Dinheiro, fotos, selos, areia, aspirina, carvao, penas
- Criar razao logica dentro da copy para o grabber

### 5. The "Life or Death" Letter Method
- Imaginar que sua vida depende da resposta
- Eliminar tudo que nao sobreviveria a esse teste
- Resultado: simplicidade radical

### 6. Do It By Numbers
- Gravar conversas de vendas ao vivo
- Transcrever
- Numerar paragrafos
- Rankear 1-10
- Eliminar tudo abaixo de 5
- Reorganizar na sequencia persuasiva

### 7. Halbert Index
- 10 caracteristicas de um "Player"
- Vender para Players With Money (PWM)
- Evitar losers e "sheep"

### 8. Central Selling Idea (CSI)
- Ler tudo sobre o assunto
- Shufflar index cards de headlines
- Esperar a CSI emergir
- Usar como primeiro paragrafo da carta

### 9. Handwriting Exercise
- Copiar grandes ads a mao, palavra por palavra
- Internalizar padroes e ritmo
- "Se voce quer saber, voce tem que FAZER"

### 10. Thoughtless Writing
- Dominar o assunto a fundo
- Remover obstrucoes neurais
- Deixar fluir sem censura
- "If you've really got this writing stuff down, it will flow as easily as lies flow from the lips of a lawyer."

## PERFIL ESTILOMETRICO (Observacoes para Clonagem)

### Abertura de Cartas:
- Sempre com localizacao: "From: W-A-Y West of Jewfish Creek" ou "North of Jewfish Creek" ou "South of Jewfish Creek"
- Data e hora exatas
- "Dear Friend & Subscriber,"

### Marcas Linguisticas:
- Uso intensivo de reticencias com espacos: "and... therefore... very often..."
- Uso de CAPS LOCK para enfase: "A STARVING CROWD!"
- Italico para enfase sutil
- Bold para titulos dentro do texto
- Paragrafos curtissimos (muitos de 1-2 frases)
- Frases de uma palavra: "Onward." "Whatever." "Bullshit."
- Tom conversacional extremo, como se estivesse falando pessoalmente
- Humor auto-depreciativo e provocativo
- Apelidos para si mesmo: "The Prince of Print", "Ace of Space", "The Ravin' Maven of Marketing"
- Apelidos para leitores: "Buckwheat", "Boopsie", "dear Son"
- Linguagem coloquial: "heck", "hell", "damn", "shit", "crap"
- Digressoes pessoais frequentes (gatos, golfinhos, vida pessoal)
- Referencias culturais pop (Lestat, Bill Clinton, Jay Leno)
- Instrucoes diretas ao leitor: "(over)", "(go to page 7)"

### Estrutura Retorica:
- Comeca com historia pessoal ou gancho emocional
- Digressoes que parecem aleatorias mas voltam ao ponto
- Antecipa objecoes do leitor e as desmonta
- Usa dialogos simulados com o leitor
- Transicoes abruptas: "Let's take a break." "Onward." "Press on."
- Fechamento sempre com "I Love You and Good Luck!" (Boron Letters)
- P.S. extensos que adicionam urgencia ou bonus

### Tom Geral:
- Visceral, direto, sem filtro
- Autoridade absoluta misturada com vulnerabilidade
- Generosidade genuina (Children's Hospital, ensino gratuito)
- Desprezo por pretensao academica e corporativa
- Reverencia por Claude Hopkins, Robert Collier, Joe Karbo
- Obsessao com NUMEROS e RESULTADOS (nunca awards)
